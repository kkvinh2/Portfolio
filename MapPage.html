Who Says it's Art?

This map visually shows the hierarchy of art present on Calle de Embajadores in Madrid. The first level is independent art, which includes graffiti and murals. It is presented in public spaces and tends to be highly accessible, allowing it to reach a broad audience. Despite its visibility and the vibrancy it adds to urban landscapes, independent art is often less respected or even stigmatized. The second level is commercial art, which encompasses posters, window displays, and advertisements. Commercial art, while often dismissed as purely functional, plays a significant role in shaping the visual landscape of Calle de Embajadores. The final layer is institutional art, which includes buildings, museums, and street signs installed by the government or other large organizations. This type of art often carries a sense of authority and permanence, symbolizing the priorities and values of the institutions behind it.
